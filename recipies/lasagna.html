Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a baked Italian dish, traditionally consisting of layers of wide, flat pasta, a meat or tomato-based sauce (often rag√π), and a creamy cheese mixture (often ricotta and mozzarella), topped with parmesan cheese. It is a hearty casserole, baked until golden brown and bubbly.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 1 cup minced onion
  • 2 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 4 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley, divided
  • 2 clove garlic, crushed
  • 1 tablespoon dried basil
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano

Steps:

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Combine pork and ground beef in a large, deep skillet over medium-high heat; cook and stir until browned and crumbly, 5 to 7 minutes.
  3. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, garlic, basil, salt, oregano, and sugar. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer, stirring occasionally, for 30 minutes.
  4. While the sauce is simmering, bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook lasagna noodles in the boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, 8 to 10 minutes. Drain and set aside. While the noodles are cooking, preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
